## üìû Common Questions & Answers

### Q: "Is curation really that important?"
**A:** Absolutely. In a world of infinite content, curation is the scarce resource. Users are overwhelmed, not underserved. Psallo's 750 songs aren't the problem‚Äîhelping users find the RIGHT 10 songs for them is the solution. That's why Spotify beat Apple Music despite identical catalogs.

### Q: "What if AI recommendations aren't good?"
**A:** That's why we have a four-layer approach. Phase 1 starts with expert curation (no AI risk). Phase 2 adds AI on top of expert foundation. Phase 3 adds community wisdom. Each layer validates and improves the others. We're not betting everything on AI‚Äîwe're building a robust system.

### Q: "How do you prevent bad community curation?"
**A:** Three mechanisms: (1) 5-star rating system so users can filter quality, (2) editorial featured collections highlighting best curators, (3) quality thresholds for featured status. We're curating the curators‚Äîmaintaining quality standards even as we scale.

### Q: "Can't competitors copy this?"
**A:** They can copy the features, but not the data, algorithms, expert network, and community trust. Curation intelligence improves with scale‚Äîthe more users, the better recommendations. That's a compounding moat. Content libraries can be replicated overnight. Curation systems take years to build.

### Q: "Why Phase 1 instead of launching AI first?"
**A:** You need data to train AI. Expert curation in Phase 1 creates the labeled dataset for AI in Phase 2. Plus, expert curation has immediate value‚Äîwe don't wait for AI to start solving the problem. It's a build-as-we-go approach.
